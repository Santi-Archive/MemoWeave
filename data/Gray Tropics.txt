Chapter 1:
 
The early morning sun glinted off the western coast of Palawan, scattering reflections across the cerulean waves. On deck of a seemingly abandoned yacht, Yann, dressed in a tourist’s minimal swimwear, scanned the horizon. She flexed her fingers, the cool metal of her concealed firearm hidden beneath the sun-bleached fabric, while her mind briefly traced weeks earlier, to the debriefing that had brought her here.


Three months prior, in a sterile briefing room marked by the sterile white of that afternoon, Yann had been handed full disclosure. The counter-intelligence agency had pulled her from the ruins of a previous mission, framing her death. It was that day she learned that her next assignment would be in the Philippines, to protect a man she didn’t yet know. The dossier had contained photographs of a local real estate agent now living in Mactan. Yann had listened, noting the details with precision, marking the date in her mind, the exact moment when her next mission became reality.


Back on the yacht, the present intruded violently. From the northern deck, shadows moved, coast guard uniforms, but too crisp, too deliberate. Yann’s instincts sharpened, immediately noting the hour, the minute, the second. The intruders were a terrorist group, masquerading as coast guards, intending to steal the yacht. The confrontation that followed was a blur of minutes, of gunshots, but Yann knew, in that very moment, that the bullets were loaded with anesthesia. The past few weeks of planning had led to this exact confrontation, the timeline of her mission converging with the unpredictable chaos of the present.


Back to three months earlier, during the debriefing, still wrapped in the sterile quiet of the recovery protocol, Yann sits across from her handler. The room carries the cold stillness of that particular week, when her body is still adjusting to the aftermath of the last covert mission that nearly cost her life. A counter-intelligence agency who had extracted her in the final minutes of that operation, framing her death only hours after stabilizing her vitals. The memory of that day lingers sharply, the heavy medical lamps, the artificial sunrise of the recovery ward, and the timeline of her survival marked by precise hours the agency logged into her report file.
Now, after reading the dossier, the handler briefs her on the mission ahead. The target she is to shadow is introduced through a series of files spread across the table. A real estate agent known publicly as Kiko Clarista, but privately identified as Lodi who was once a local bounty hunter, now managing a network of listing properties inherited from his uncle. The handler explains that these properties hide military-grade gadgetry created by Kiko himself, an inheritance of skill from his late father. These details, gathered over months of intelligence work, define the framework of Yann’s future operation.


Back to the present day, beneath the thick mid-day heat of Mactan, Yann arrives at the airport. The current hour is coated in tropical humidity as she meets Monica and Sarah, her assigned informants already disguised as casual tourists. They update her with data compiled over the last several weeks. The data includes Kiko’s daily itinerary, his movements across the country, and the pattern of his daily routines. Now, Yann learns this new information threaded with surveillance timestamps collected in real time.


Elsewhere in the present day, three NIS field agents check into available listing properties in Mactan. They do so under the guise of foreign visitors, fitting themselves into the tourist-heavy hours of the afternoon check-in rush. They begin observing the immediate environment, noting the building layout and potential vantage points. Their real mission is intended to be done on the same day in the attempt to track down the same target. That target is revealed to be Kiko as if no one will notice the following day of his whereabouts.


Meanwhile, Kiko travels across the Philippines on a personal visit to his uncle’s listing properties in the past several weeks. His trip covers Taguig, Subic and Davao unbeknownst to the management and staff who also worked under his uncle's management. His visits follow a schedule his uncle established years ago, but he performs them now with an added layer of caution built over the past two years. Now, his later visits are greeted with an unease that has quietly grown with every irregularity he notices.




Back at the debriefing, Yann asks her handler why Kiko personally travels to each location. The handler reviews prior intelligence logs compiled over the past year, explaining that Kiko’s hidden gadgetry is distributed beneath several of those properties, embedded into infrastructures he modified after his father’s passing. This information had surfaced from former informants months earlier, which prompted renewed surveillance and the reassignment of the mission to Yann, working along with Monica and Sarah.


Returning to the present, Monica and Sarah outline the background of Kiko’s uncle, known to them through research conducted over previous years. The uncle, Tito, was once a military lawyer before shifting into civilian legal practice and later entrepreneurship. After the death of Kiko’s father years earlier, Tito retired from military involvement and eventually built a business incubator in Mactan, taking over Kiko’s responsibilities following Kiko's house arrest two years prior. These details form the backbone of the counter-intelligence team's understanding of Kiko’s unique skill set.


Later that afternoon, the NIS agents pursue Kiko’s location through airport surveillance. Their digital tracking began soon after their arrival but is disrupted minutes later by Kiko’s AI-driven deepfake interference system. The software confuses their feed with multiple false matches of individuals resembling Kiko, scattering their search across the terminal. By the end of the hour, they lose his trail entirely.


Moments later, on his way toward his residence, Kiko receives a call from Tito. The uncle asks him to drop by the newly renovated establishment, now renamed “Tito’s.” As Kiko reviews the property data during the drive, he begins to notice patterns within the last two years. He finds out an unusual rise in inquiries from foreign tourists across multiple listings, far exceeding the typical demographic spread after he was assigned to manage his uncle’s properties two years prior. His data visualizations reveal spikes and clusters that do not align with ordinary market behavior, comparing the majority demographics of local tourists years before his assignment as business manager.


This discovery pushes Kiko into deeper suspicion. The patterns feel orchestrated, as though shaped by an external hand. His thoughts drift across the past two years, moments he once dismissed as coincidences and now reframed under this sudden clarity.


Chapter 2: 


Before he can reach the roadhouse, Kiko’s screen lights up with a text from an unknown number. The message is urgent, instructing him to visit someone residing in one of the listing properties under his management. The timestamp of the message, the anonymity, and its precise reference to his routes all heighten the tension surrounding his next move.


Near evening, Yann, Monica, and Sarah moved toward one of Tito’s listed properties where they planned to stay for the next few nights. As they crossed the parking area of the property, Sarah brushed past a traveler who appeared to be another tourist, though the encounter happened too neatly for coincidence. Only minutes later, the person she bumped into had been one of the disguised NIS agents already stationed in the area.


Earlier that same evening, before the trio arrived, the NIS agents were conducting their own quiet sweep of the property. By the time Sarah had bumped into one of them, they had just discovered that one of their local-made tracker chips had gone missing. The realization forced them to retrace their steps throughout the entire rental property. They checked corners, shelves, vents, and furniture, all while racing against the possibility that someone else might locate the missing device before they did. Their urgency grew with every passing minute of that tense early evening.


Elsewhere at the same hour, Kiko followed the coordinates sent to him by the unknown text earlier that afternoon. When he reached the listed property indicated by the message, he found another bounty hunter waiting for Kira, a former associate he had interacted with years ago. Her presence meant the situation had escalated beyond routine surveillance. According to her, she had left Incheon only hours before she would have been captured by NIS agents who had already begun closing in on her identity.
Back at Yann’s rental stay, she unpacked and sorted through the items in her small field bag, she discovered a tracker chip nestled inside one of Sarah’s compartments. The discovery occurring just before the trio prepared for dinner shifted their operational mindset. The possibility that they had been monitored by an unwanted enemy. Yann reviewed the tracker chip’s placement, examined its construction, and attempted to determine whether their current mission had already been compromised. The uncertainty lingered through each moment of preparation.


Minutes later, Yann, Monica, and Sarah left the rental unit with a heightened awareness of their surroundings. Their objective shifted into a dual operation: gather intelligence on Kiko while also identifying whoever might be tracking them. As they stepped outside, they unexpectedly crossed paths with Kiko and Kira heading toward their own destination. The brief overlap lasted only a moment, but it was enough for the trio to react. They moved quickly toward their car to avoid losing sight of the pair.


The trio’s route eventually led them to Tito’s roadhouse. While approaching the entrance, they continued scanning the environment for any signs of the unknown party monitoring their movements. Each second felt elongated by the weight of potential compromise. Still, their assignment required them to prioritize gathering information as Kiko approached Tito for a discussion that would likely reveal data relevant to the last two years of monitoring.


Inside the roadhouse, in a private area separated from the dinner crowd, Tito’s staff informed Kiko that his uncle was busy giving a tour to a group of foreigners. Once Tito finished the brief interaction and shifted into a more private setting, Kiko presented his findings from his accumulated data. Over the past two years, foreign tourist inquiries at his uncle’s properties had risen sharply, then stabilized suddenly after a single month of abnormal fluctuations. Tito believed the upward trend existed due to Kiko’s improvements in digitalization. However, Kiko’s long-term analysis from previous months suggested different patterns, signals that implied unusual external interest rather than organic digital growth.




Outside the private room, Yann, Monica, and Sarah monitored multiple channels at once. Their present time was divided between observing Tito and Kiko’s private meeting from a distance and assessing whether their own movements were being tracked by someone nearby. They coordinated silently, cross-checking signals, scanning the environment, and maintaining awareness of every passerby in that moment. The operation required them to split attention between immediate intelligence gathering and long-term situational safety.


Meanwhile, back at the rental stay, the NIS agents struggled to locate their missing listening device. Their efforts became increasingly frantic until one of them repaired the master receiver unit that had been hacked earlier at the airport. Once the device was functional again, they accessed the tracking interface that revealed the locations of each tracker chip The missing one, according to the restored system, was no longer within the property. It was now transmitting from Tito’s roadhouse.


As Kiko sat in the private space across from Tito, he equipped his anti-radiation glasses. The device, created years earlier with his father’s technological foundation, allowed him to view real-time radiation signatures and digital signals invisible to ordinary equipment. Through these lenses, he attempted to determine whether his suspicions about external surveillance were correct. The demographic spike from previous years, the abrupt stabilization after a single month, and the expanding presence of foreign visitors aligned too neatly with hidden infrared signatures detected through the glasses. Yet he remained uncertain about who was conducting the monitoring, as no single country or agency pattern matched the recent two-year trend.


Nearby in the same room, sitting alone while maintaining her own defensive stance, Kira observed movements around the roadhouse. She noticed Monica and Sarah occasionally adjusting their positions, leaning closer toward the hallway leading to Kiko’s conversation with Tito. Their presence at those particular moments hinted that they were attempting to eavesdrop, prompting Kira to assess them as potential threats or rival operatives.




At that same moment, Yann, positioned to cover both Monica and Sarah, noticed Kira’s attention turning toward their location. The timing of her glances, the subtle adjustments of her posture, and her strategic seating arrangement caused Yann to suspect that Kira might be connected to the monitoring activity they had detected earlier that night. This suspicion grew stronger as Yann tracked the timing of Kira’s reactions against the spatial layout of the roadhouse and the alignment of the tracker chip signal.


Chapter 3: 


Three months earlier, back at the debriefing room, Yann faced the glowing monitor where her handler pulled up a new file. When the image of Kira appeared, the handler explained her background, beginning decades earlier and spanning two countries long before Yann had ever been assigned to her mission.


Years before Kira had even existed, her father had fled the North Korean border of the Korean peninsula, seeking a life beyond the patterns of scarcity he had known since childhood. Sometime after arriving in the South, however, he had been deceived by men promising legitimate work. Soon afterward, his unplanned involvement in a violent underground dispute placed him directly in the sights of a mafia syndicate that operated beneath the normal flow of city life.


Later, after surviving a brutal night that left nearly everyone else dead, he found unexpected rescue when Kira’s Japanese grandfather, Shiro, sent his own men to recover him. In the months following that rescue, the older man allowed his daughter to marry the survivor, though even then he set a condition long rooted in the family’s history: the next generation would continue the syndicate’s cross-generational business. As time moved forward, the marriage produced a son, then eventually Kira herself.


Over the years, the family shifted between South Korea and Japan while Shiro  maintained the clan’s structure. But Kira’s brother, Atari, in his late teens at the time, attempted to claim power prematurely. His actions led to the deaths of his own parents which was an outcome he had not planned, however reckless his ambition. Soon after, he vanished from the organization altogether, never seen again. In the years that followed, Kira grew up under Shiro's strict control. Eventually, just before her early rebellious adulthood began, she escaped, disappearing into the international underground world the agency had been observing for months. 


Now, back in the present, the humid night around Tito’s roadhouse carried the faint noise of traffic and distant music. Moments earlier, Yann had slipped away from her surveillance spot long enough to approach Kira discreetly. In this current moment, both women weighed what the other represented. Yann understood that revealing her intentions was the only way to settle the immediate tension. Kira, in turn, measured whether cooperation would give her temporary safety from the NIS agents who had been chasing her since her departure from Incheon.


Not long after their silent exchange of terms, they separated just before Kiko would spot the both of them. The timing was narrow enough that neither Monica nor Sarah needed to intervene. For the rest of the evening, the trio continued their surveillance while discreetly observing whether Kira upheld her side of their agreement.


Inside the roadhouse a short time later, Kiko and Tito’s conversation shifted sharply when the two noticed several rude caucasian tourists harassing a newly hired staff member. Kiko acted immediately, guiding the drunk visitors outside the establishment. Once they were outside, he judged that their intoxicated state made escalation pointless, yet their behavior still required a firm boundary. A few controlled movements later, he left one tourist with a broken nose and another with a fractured arm.


Tito stepped in before the situation grew worse, handing Kiko the keys to his van. Though reluctant at the moment, Kiko proceeded to drive the injured tourists toward a nearby hospital. Kira followed, sensing that the night’s timing was moving into dangerous territory. Neither of them saw the NIS agent crouched nearby, slipping a tracker beneath the van’s rear panel.




But as the agents attempted to tail the van, they were spotted by Yann, Monica, and Sarah along with former informants who had been monitoring Kiko for the past two years. For a moment, the road outside the roadhouse became a moving puzzle of watchers, followed by watchers, followed by watchers.


Kiko and Kira arrive at the hospital under the dim glow of early evening, carrying the weight of the injured drunkards they had brought minutes away to the hospital. As Kira guides the stumbling men into the emergency room, Kiko remains by the entrance, scanning the surroundings with the same vigilance he has carried for the past two years. Only now, in this precise instant, his attention sharpens, his DIY tracking device, placed in his uncle’s van weeks earlier as a precaution, flashes with a sudden alert.


Seconds later, he realizes a tracking chip has been embedded somewhere on the vehicle without his knowledge. This realization forces him to act immediately. He signals Kira just moments before anyone in the hospital corridor notices their urgency. Within that same moment, both of them rush out of the building, determined to return to the property where Kira has been staying before anyone trailing them closes in. The present now becomes a race against unseen pursuers.


Meanwhile, at the property where Yann, Monica, and Sarah are temporarily staying earlier, the informants maintain control over the restrained NIS field agents. Hours earlier, the trio had discovered the NIS monitoring bugs, but only now do they attempt to extract answers. Under pressure, one of the NIS agents finally breaks, revealing that their team is in Mactan because Kiko is hiding Kira. An individual Kiko was supposed to return to Shiro when he was still operating as a bounty hunter two years ago. The statement creates a ripple of realization among the informants and the trio, but they have no time to explore it further. Presently, the interrogation abruptly ends as Kiko and Kira arrive at the edge of the property.


In that moment, the trio and the informants scatter into hiding, attempting to conceal both themselves and the captives. Kira, sensing danger, quickly distracts Kiko outside the entrance, delaying him just long enough for everyone inside to find cover. The trio and the informants scramble through the hallways, searching for a place to conceal the NIS agents. Only after several panicked seconds do they stumble upon a room filled with servers, surveillance equipment, and rows of unfinished gadgetry hidden under sheets. It becomes clear that this property contains more than a typical rental listing.


Chapter 4: 


Back at the debriefing set three months earlier, Yann had asked her handler what kind of military-grade technology Kiko was protecting. At the time, her handler revealed that Kiko’s father had once worked for the CIA as an engineer. Years before Kiko’s current life, his father had created a secret project designed to keep his family safe after realizing that his inventions had reached unwanted rival groups. That classified project, now rediscovered through this server room, was an AI-driven hive-minded drone system intended to act as a protective shield. Yann had been warned then that the surviving prototypes were still hidden beneath Kiko’s network of inherited properties.


Returning to the present moment, the situation escalates once more. The NIS agents, having slipped their restraints, break free and seal the trio and informants inside the server room. The lock snaps shut just seconds before alarms begin to hum through the walls. Outside, before they can exit the property, Kiko and Kira intercept the escaping NIS agents. The confrontation turns physical, and amidst the chaos, one NIS operative accidentally triggers an intruder alert on the control panel. The alert pulses through the system, activating at the same time Tito notices the alarm back at the roadhouse.


As the alarm echoes through the server room, the hive-minded drones activate. The trio and informants scramble for cover as the drones begin firing into the locked door, attempting to force an exit. The air fills with the sound of mechanical targeting as the drones calculate the quickest way out.


At the lobby, Kiko and Kira overwhelm the NIS agents and finally neutralize them. Kiko, aware that every second counts, powers on the full server system. Kira, now fully understanding the danger of their situation, opens the server room door the moment the drones pierce through. The three AI-driven military-grade drones hover upward, emerging into open space with eerie coordination.


The trio and their informants regain control over the NIS agents in the aftermath, but their attention shifts as soon as they realize Kiko has vanished once again. Only minutes later, as Tito arrives at the exterior of his property, he spots Kiko standing in the open night, illuminated by the soft glow of his van’s headlights. Kiko wears his anti-radiant glasses, revealing to him an augmented view of digital indicators surrounding multiple drones. Behind him, the drones hover silently, confirming Kiko’s earlier suspicions that the unwanted enemies are monitoring actions for the past two years.


Inside the server room, the remaining screens flicker to life, revealing synchronized maps of the Philippines. In that present second, it becomes clear there are not just three drones in Mactan. There are more scattered across multiple regions of the country, awaiting activation. The truth, long buried in both past and present, finally reveals its scale.


Shifting back three months before the present mission, where Yann sits in the dim briefing chamber still marked by the injuries she sustained only weeks earlier. At this earlier moment, the sterile room feels heavier than usual, carrying the weight of the mission yet to unfold. Yann studies her handler while quietly questioning why she, still recovering, is being selected for an assignment that could easily have been given to someone fully fit.


Moments later, the handler explains the decision with a calm certainty built from years of observing both Yann and the target she will soon protect. The handler has accumulated extensive intelligence over the years, not only on Kiko’s movements but also on his life before his father’s death, his past entanglements with his uncle’s business, and the dangerous encounters he faced during his work as the elusive bounty hunter known as Lodi. The handler’s familiarity with Kiko’s history stretches back far longer than Yann had expected.


As Yann listens, a recollection emerges from two years earlier, during the period when Kiko was placed under house arrest. In that same time, Yann had been on assignment in the same region, witnessing from the periphery the tension surrounding Kiko’s confinement. 


Minutes later in the briefing room, Yann asks how the handler managed to gather such comprehensive intelligence spanning so many years. The handler reveals, with a steady composure, that she herself had been in charge during Kiko’s house arrest two years prior. This statement unsettles Yann just enough for an earlier memory to resurface, an earlier image of a woman overseeing Kiko’s confinement from behind a reinforced observation window. Now, in the present briefing, recognition forms in her mind.


It becomes clear at this moment: the handler is Kiko’s mother.


The revelation settles into Yann’s awareness gradually, filling the room with a quiet gravity. The handler, aware of the emotional weight, places a stack of documents and reading materials onto the table reports, timelines, surveillance logs, psychological evaluations, and the entire chronology of Kiko’s life from childhood to the present day. These materials, gathered over years, define the foundation of the mission.


Hours later, Yann sits alone in her quarters, reading through the handler’s records. Each file traces a different moment in Kiko’s past: the years before his father’s death, the turbulent period after, the months tied to his uncle’s ventures, and the two-year span marked by hidden surveillance and scattered clues that now seem to point toward something larger. Every detail reveals why the agency has monitored him for so long.


Finally, hours later, she delivers her decision. The acceptance comes quietly, at a moment when she recognizes the mission’s depth, its connections across years, and the unspoken expectations placed upon her since the beginning. The earlier uncertainty dissolves, replaced by the resolve she carries into the present timeline of Mactan, where everything now converges.
